Research & Projects

Divergence-Guided RL for LLM Reasoning (DivGRPO)2025 – present · lead
  • Identified a core failure of GRPO: a trajectory's final-outcome reward is broadcast to all tokens, mis-crediting correct steps in incorrect trajectories and rewarding lucky steps in correct ones — empirically linked to entropy instability (collapse / explosion) on distilled reasoning models.
  • Proposed DivGRPO: an asymmetric, multiplicative reweighting of the GRPO advantage at token-level divergence points — penalty peaks at the divergence point and decays exponentially; correct paths reinforced afterward.
  • Implemented on verl; controlled multi-seed experiments across DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B / 7B and Qwen3-4B on 8×A800.
  • Result: stable low-entropy convergence where GRPO is unstable — at 1.5B preserves AIME24 acc@12 ≈ 0.23 (vs. GRPO ≈ 0.16 under entropy explosion); at 7B holds macro accuracy ≈ 0.53 throughout, avoiding GRPO's late-stage degradation.
LLM from scratch: pre-training → SFT → RLHFcode
  • Implemented Transformer, tokenizer, and the full pipeline from zero; pre-training, SFT, and PPO/GRPO alignment — end-to-end intuition for reward design, KL control, and entropy monitoring.
Reproducible RL training & evaluation infrastructure (verl)

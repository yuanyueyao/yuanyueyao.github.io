About

I am an incoming M.S. student in Computer Science at Zhejiang University (Sept 2026), advised by Prof. Zhe Liu and co-advised by Prof. Xiaogang Xu. My research centers on the post-training of large language models for reasoning — reinforcement learning, self-distillation, reward design, credit assignment, and training stability.

I received my B.Eng. from Shandong University, where I worked on retrieval-augmented generation and tool retrieval for LLM agents. I have also implemented an LLM from scratch (pre-training → SFT → RLHF).

Research & Projects

Divergence-Guided RL for LLM Reasoning (DivGRPO)2025 – present · lead
  • Studying credit assignment in GRPO, where a trajectory's final-outcome reward is broadcast to all tokens — mis-crediting correct steps in incorrect trajectories and lucky steps in correct ones.
  • Proposing DivGRPO: an asymmetric, multiplicative reweighting of the GRPO advantage at token-level divergence points between correct/incorrect trajectories, in pure token space. Implemented on verl with multi-seed experiments on distilled reasoning models.
Tool-retrieval benchmark for LLM agents2025
  • Co-built the first realistic tool-retrieval benchmark — a 43k-tool dataset with diverse retrieval tasks — to measure and improve how IR models pre-filter tools for LLM agents.
  • Evaluated sparse, dense, and re-ranking retrievers. Open-sourced; 170+ GitHub stars.
Social-risk early-warning system for Jinan2024 · lead
  • With SDU's School of Political Science & Public Administration and the Jinan authorities, built a visual-analytics system over ~1.22M civic records (mediation, courts, petitions, 12345 hotline) to support governance decisions.
  • Fine-tuned Llama 3 with domain QA + RAG to extract dispute events and generate risk reports with reduced hallucination; built BERT dual-tower semantic retrieval for domain-specific search.
Pose-driven portrait attribute transfer via diffusion2025 – present · lead
  • Building a diffusion-based method (on Wan 2.1, with self-reconstruction training) that turns a single portrait, a reference image, and a facial-keypoint video into multi-attribute-editable, multi-view-coherent animation — moving portrait attribute transfer from static images to dynamic expression and pose.
